Garlic Bread

Description

Garlic bread is a popular side dish made with slices of bread (typically baguette or Italian bread) topped with a savory mixture of butter, garlic, and herbs. It's baked until crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, often served with pasta, soups, or as an appetizer.

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf of French baguette or Italian bread
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter (softened)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional: grated Parmesan or mozzarella cheese for topping

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Slice the bread into 1-inch thick pieces.
  3. Make garlic butter: In a small bowl, mix softened butter, minced garlic, parsley, salt, and olive oil.
  4. Spread mixture generously onto each slice of bread.
  5. (Optional) Sprinkle cheese on top for extra flavor.
  6. Arrange slices on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  7. Bake for 10–12 minutes or until edges are golden brown. For extra crispiness, broil for 1–2 minutes at the end.
  8. Serve warm as a side or appetizer.
