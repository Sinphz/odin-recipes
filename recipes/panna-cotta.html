Panna Cotta

Description

Panna Cotta is a classic Italian dessert known for its silky, creamy texture. The name means "cooked cream" in Italian. It's typically flavored with vanilla and served chilled, often with a fruit sauce or fresh berries.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (500 ml) heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) whole milk
  • 1/2 cup (100 g) granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract (or 1 vanilla bean)
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract (or 1 vanilla bean)
  • 2 ½ teaspoons (1 packet) unflavored gelatin
  • 3 tablespoons cold water

Steps

  1. Bloom the Gelatin: In a small bowl, sprinkle gelatin over the cold water and let it sit for 5–10 minutes to soften.
  2. Heat the Cream Mixture: In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, milk, and sugar. Heat over medium heat, stirring until sugar dissolves and mixture is hot (do not boil)
  3. Add Gelatin & Vanilla: Remove from heat. Stir in the bloomed gelatin until completely dissolved. Then add the vanilla extract (or scrape the seeds from a vanilla bean into the mixture).
  4. Pour into Molds: Strain the mixture through a fine sieve if desired, then pour into lightly greased ramekins or molds.
  5. Chill: Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or until fully set.
  6. Serve: Unmold onto plates and top with berry sauce and mint if using.
